Designing a digital crowd-engagement platform, created to foster mass participation.

Background

Our client is a startup that aims to create an online platform for idea generation and collaboration. The website will be targeted at entrepreneurs, inventors, and anyone with a creative mind who wants to collaborate with others to turn their ideas into reality. Our goal is to create a user-friendly and visually appealing website that promotes idea sharing and collaboration.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Year

2021

Software

Figma

Research

User Interviews

To understand our users, we conducted user interviews with entrepreneurs, inventors, and creative individuals who had experience with ideation and collaboration. We found that our users were looking for a website that was easy to use, allowed them to connect with other ideators, and provided them with tools to help them refine their ideas. They also wanted a way to share their ideas without the risk of them being stolen.

Competitive Analysis

There are several collaboration websites available in the market that cater to ideators, including: IdeaScale, MindMeister, MURAL, Aha and Slack. Each of these collaboration websites offers unique features and benefits to ideators. IdeaScale and Aha! are more focused on idea management and strategy, while MindMeister and MURAL provide visual collaboration tools. Slack, on the other hand, is more focused on real-time communication and team collaboration. To stand out in the competitive market, a collaboration website for ideators needs to offer a unique value proposition, such as stronger security measures for idea protection, or a more user-friendly interface.

User Persona

Based on our research, we created a user persona for our target users, Janet. Janet is a marketing manager for a tech startup in the Bay Area. Janet is highly creative and is always coming up with new ideas for her company. She's an ideator who loves to brainstorm and collaborate with others to bring her ideas to life. She's been looking for a platform where she can share her ideas and connect with like-minded individuals who can help her bring her ideas to life.

How to address these challenges:

  1. Make the platform easy to use and navigate.
  2. Ensure that the platform has strong security measures in place to protect users' ideas.
  3. Create a community-focused platform that fosters collaboration and connection.
  4. Provide resources to help users connect with other ideators who have complementary skill sets.
  5. Offer features like idea ownership and non-disclosure agreements to help users feel more secure about sharing their ideas.
  6. Provide a mobile app that allows Janet to access the platform on-the-go.

Wireframes

We created wireframes to visualize the layout of the website. We used a simple and clean design with a focus on usability. The wireframes included clear call-to-action buttons, easy navigation, and a consistent color scheme.

Mockups

Once the wireframes were finalized, we created high-fidelity mockups to showcase the final design of the website. The mockups included images and text that reflected the startup's branding and message.

Development

Front-End Development

We developed the website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. We used responsive design to ensure that the website was optimized for different devices and screen sizes.

Back-End Development

We developed a back-end system using a database to store user profiles, ideas, and collaborations. We also developed a system to allow users to protect their ideas through a non-disclosure agreement.

Testing

Usability Testing

We conducted usability testing with a group of ideators to ensure that the website was easy to use and met their needs. We collected feedback on the design, functionality, and user experience of the website.

User Acceptance Testing

We also conducted user acceptance testing with a group of ideators to ensure that the website met their expectations and was useful for finding collaborators, refining ideas, and protecting their intellectual property.

Conclusion

In conclusion, we created an ideator website for our startup client that met the needs of our target users. The website was easy to use, allowed ideators to connect with each other, and provided tools to help them refine their ideas. The website also allowed ideators to protect their intellectual property through a non-disclosure agreement. Overall, the website was a success and met the goals of our startup client.

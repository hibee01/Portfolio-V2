CUBA MOBILE BANKING

Digital banking for the modern age

Overview

Cuba is a platform that understands the benefits that digital transformation has to offer to the banking and financial industry. It aims to spearhead the next generation of banking services, both in Africa and elsewhere. Cuba's goal is to make sure its users have access to their money and perform various tasks which include but are not limited to checking deposits, bill payments, loan applications, money transfers between accounts from any part of the world, etc. The purpose of this project is to build a scalable mobile platform on which to fully digitize all banking services and offerings.

Design Phase

Reasearch

The goal was to understand the audiences and learn about their backgrounds, demographics, motivations, pain points, and emotions and also use a survey to verify how and in what frequency audiences use similar platforms. A competitive analysis paired with previous research gave me more insights to sketch the prototypes for the consumer app.

Personas

After consolidating and analyzing the research data gathered, I came up with 3 personas of fictional users whose goals and characteristics represent the needs of a larger group of users These personas allow me to constantly be reminded of the people that Iâ€™m designing the product for, their needs and wants, as well as pain points.

Ideation & Prototype

After generating a broad set of ideas and evaluating them I was able to select the best of them for inclusion in the product. Based on my choice of concept, I created a prototype for user testing. The prototype also allowed me to test navigation and usability internally, as well as quickly come up with a basic design language to govern how components and layouts should look and flow on different screens.

User Testing

I carried out user testing using the prototype to hear users' feedback on the usability, and navigation of expected features from a mobile banking application, and conduct A/B testing on elements such as icon interpretation, UX writing, and color palette. Based on the data collected during user testing, I was able to make a couple of iterative improvements in between these main iterations, but they primarily dealt with color changes, icon style, content display, layout expectations, and dashboard personalizations based on individual needs.
